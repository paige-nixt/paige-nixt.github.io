Cast Iron Skillet Cornbread

Recipe adapted from the Food Charlatan

Photo by Adam Bartoszewicz on Unsplash

YIELD:

Serves 8-9 people

TIME:

35-40 minutes

Dry Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups of yellow corn meal
  • 1/2 teaspoon of sea salt
  • 1 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 2 tesaspoons of baking powder

Wet Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1 tablespoon of vinegar
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) of butter
  • 1/4 cup of canola oil
  • 3/4 cup of cane sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • 2 large eggs

Optional Add-ins:

  • Jalepenos and cheddar cheese
  • Blueberries and mashed banana

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit and place a 9-inch cast iron skillet in the oven while the oven preheats.
  2. Make the sour milk by combining 1 cup milk and 1 tablespoon of vinegar, stir a little and set aside.
  3. In a small bowl, combine the dry ingredients (flour, cornmeal, salt, baking soda and powder).
  4. Cut off ½ a tablespoon of butter and set aside. Melt the remaining stick of butter in a large mixing bowl.
  5. Add oil, sugar, and honey to the melted butter. Stir until well combined.
  6. Add 2 eggs and the sour milk made in step 2. Whisk until incorporated.
  7. Using a spatula, stir the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients. Stir until just combined, the batter will be lumpy. DO NOT OVER MIX!
  8. Remove the skillet from the oven and melt the ½ a tablespoon of butter in the skillet you set aside earlier.
  9. Pour the batter into the greased skillet and shake a little to even out the batter.
  10. Place the skillet back into the oven and bake for 28-32 minutes. The cornbread is done when the top is golden brown and the center is firm. If you insert a toothpick, it should come out clean.
  11. Let cool for 5 minutes before serving!
  12. The cornbread can be stored in a covered container at room temperature for up to 3 days.
